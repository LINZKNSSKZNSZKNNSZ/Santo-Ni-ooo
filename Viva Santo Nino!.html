About

• Sinulog Festival is a religious festival dedicated to Sto. Niño. This is held during the 3rd Sunday of January. People come to celebrate this festival to honour Sto. Niño by doing all sorts of activities such as conducting a mass, having a grand parade and people shouting out the chant "Viva Pit Señor."

History

• The Sinulog Festival originated from Cebu and was first celebrated in 1521. During the time when Ferdinand Magellan arrived in Cebu, the Sinulog Festival was celebrated in order to honour Sto. Niño or the Child Jesus and to spread C hristianity all over the Philippines. The name of the festival originated from the Cebuano word sulog that means "water current."

More

• Sinulog Festival is known for its variety of activities that are being done. Having a grand parade, shouting out the chant "Viva Pit Señor," street dancing, and many more.
